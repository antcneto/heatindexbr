Heat index climatology
for Brazil's Semiarid

1,477 municipalities · 288 month×hour combinations · 2000-2025 · ~1 km resolution

Spatially interpolated Heat Index (Rothfusz 1990) climatology for the official Brazilian Semiarid region (CONDEL/SUDENE Res. 176/2024). Covers 12 months × 24 hours (2000-2025) using kriging with external drift, with covariate selection validated per combination via leave-one-out cross-validation. Mean R² = 0.778, RMSE = 1.12°C across 288 combinations.

The data

A climatology, not a single year

Each of the 288 rasters represents the 2000-2025 mean Heat Index for one month and one UTC hour, giving a much more robust picture of typical thermal conditions than any single year could. The interpolation method varies by combination: KDE with altitude dominates at night, while ERA5-based covariates dominate during daytime hours, when synoptic forcing is stronger.

Criterion: R² ≥ 0.70 + lowest RMSE. KDE with altitude dominates at night (blue); ERA5-based covariates dominate during the day (orange/red).

Predicted IC (1 km, upscaled to 55 km) vs MERRA-2 reanalysis (~55 km), independent of the interpolation model. R² = 0.813, Bias = -0.43°C.

Why trust the raster?

Validated combination by combination

Each of the 288 month × hour combinations was independently cross-validated using leave-one-out cross-validation (LOOCV) against 119 INMET stations. The winning interpolation method was selected per combination, not globally, because the dominant physical process changes throughout the day.

Method varies by physical regime
KDE with altitude alone wins at night (00h-06h UTC), when synoptic forcing is weak. ERA5-based covariates dominate during daytime hours (10h-20h UTC), when regional atmospheric forcing drives temperature more than local relief.
✓
Independent of MERRA-2 model assumptions
MERRA-2 is a global reanalysis product, entirely independent of the INMET station network used to build this climatology. Strong agreement (R² = 0.813) at the upscaled 55 km resolution supports the physical plausibility of the 1 km product.
✓
Low-confidence combinations are flagged
58 of 288 combinations fall below R² = 0.70, concentrated in March-June around 06h-09h UTC, a transitional period between rainy and dry seasons. These are flagged internally and the package warns users when such a combination is requested.

Get started

Four lines to your first result

hi_download()
Download the climatology stack (cached after first use) and extract any month × hour combination.
hi_municipality()
Fast lookup from the pre-computed municipal table. No raster download needed.
hi_shape()
Extract statistics for any custom sf polygon.
hi_search()
Search for municipalities by name or state.
hi_plot()
Plot a raster or municipality result as a ggplot map.
hi_available()
List available products, months, and hours.
# Install from GitHub # remotes::install_github("antcneto/heatindexbr") library(heatindexbr) # Fast: municipal table, no raster download hi_municipality("MOSSORO", state = "RN", month = 1, hour_local = 15) # Single raster band (downloads stack once, cached after) r <- hi_download(month = 1, hour_local = 15) hi_plot(r) # Legacy 2025 synoptic product (v0.1.0) hi_download(product = "synoptic_2025", hour_local = 15)

Hours are specified in local time (UTC-3) and converted automatically. Municipality names follow IBGE convention: uppercase without accents. Use hi_search() to find the correct name.
