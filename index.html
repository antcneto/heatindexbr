The data

Heat index across the Semiarid

Annual mean Heat Index for 2025, mapped at ~1 km resolution across all 1,477 municipalities of Brazil's official Semiarid region. Four synoptic hours capture the full thermal cycle: nocturnal minimum, morning warming, afternoon peak, and evening cooling.

Annual mean Heat Index at 15h · KDE with external drift: altitude + ERA5-Land T2m · INMET 2025 · EPSG:5880

NOAA classification at 15h · 640 municipalities under extreme caution (IC 32 to 41°C) · 13.3 million people

Daily cycle: midnight, 09h, 15h, 21h local time · Covariates vary by hour: NDVI at night, ERA5 at 15h

Population exposure

31 million people under heat stress

At 15h, annual mean Heat Index classifies 96.8% of the Semiarid population under NOAA caution or extreme caution, based on 2022 IBGE Census data. Annual means are a conservative lower bound: daily peaks in specific months can exceed IC > 41°C.

Why trust the raster?

Rigorous interpolation, verified results

Fifteen spatial interpolation methods were compared across all four synoptic hours via leave-one-out cross-validation (LOOCV). The winning method, kriging with external drift (KDE), selects covariates per hour based on physical justification: NDVI captures nocturnal land-surface processes in the Caatinga; ERA5 captures regional synoptic forcing at 15h.

✓
Spatially unbiased residuals
Variogram analysis shows a pure nugget effect (Sill = 0, Nugget ≈ 1.05). Moran's I = -0.027, p = 0.864: residuals are spatially random, confirming the model absorbs all systematic spatial structure.
✓
Covariates are physically independent
Partial correlations on 100,000 pixels: IC × ERA5 | altitude = 0.977; IC × altitude | ERA5 = -0.929. The raster is not redundant with an elevation model: both covariates capture distinct physical processes.
✓
Random Forest rejected after spatial CV
Block spatial CV revealed leakage in RF (delta R² = 0.18 at 21h, 0.08 at 15h). KDE R² is stable across both LOOCV and block CV, confirming genuine spatial generalization.

Get started

Four lines to your first map

hi_download()
Download and cache the raster from Zenodo. Returns a SpatRaster.
hi_municipality()
Extract Heat Index stats for one or more municipalities by name.
hi_shape()
Extract stats for any custom sf polygon.
hi_search()
Search for municipalities by name or state.
hi_plot()
Plot a raster or municipality result as a ggplot map.
hi_available()
List available years, resolutions, and hours.
# Install from GitHub # remotes::install_github("antcneto/heatindexbr") library(heatindexbr) # Search for a municipality (names follow IBGE uppercase convention) hi_search("Mossoro", state = "RN") # Extract annual mean Heat Index at 15h hi_municipality("MOSSORO", state = "RN", hour = "15h") # Download the full raster and plot r <- hi_download(hour = "15h") hi_plot(r)

Municipality names follow IBGE convention: uppercase without accents. Use hi_search() to find the correct name (e.g. MOSSORO, not Mossoró).
